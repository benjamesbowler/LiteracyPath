## gs-b-01: The Kite in the Wind

- Level: B
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Target literacy focus: blends, digraph review, problem/solution with friends
- Target vocabulary: kite, wind, string, stuck, branch, help
- High-frequency words: the, my, said, come, here, help
- Moral/theme: Friends can solve a problem safely together.
- Source inspiration: Original LiteracyPath story

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Lena ran across the field with her yellow kite. The wind pulled the string high.

### Page 2

Omar clapped as the kite dipped and rose. The red tail flipped in the wind.

### Page 3

A strong gust pushed the kite toward the oak tree. Lena held the spool tight.

### Page 4

The kite stuck on a branch. Lena tugged the string, but the kite did not move.

### Page 5

"Stop tugging," said Omar. "The string may snap." Lena took one step back.

### Page 6

Omar found a long fallen stick. He lifted the string while Lena held the spool still.

### Page 7

The kite slid down from the branch. Lena caught it before it touched the grass.

### Page 8

Lena smiled at Omar. The kite flew again, and this time they watched the tree.

## gs-b-02: The Fox and the Grapes

- Level: B
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Target literacy focus: long vowels, simple dialogue, honest feelings
- Target vocabulary: fox, grapes, high, jump, sour, honest
- High-frequency words: the, said, look, good, not, away
- Moral/theme: It is better to be honest than to pretend.
- Source inspiration: Public-domain Aesop adaptation, rewritten by LiteracyPath

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Finn the fox saw grapes on a high vine. The grapes looked round and sweet.

### Page 2

Finn jumped once. His paws did not touch the grapes.

### Page 3

He jumped again and landed in the dust. The grapes stayed high.

### Page 4

A bluebird sat on the fence. "Those grapes look good," said the bird.

### Page 5

Finn stood tall and flicked his tail. "They are sour," he said.

### Page 6

Finn walked away, but he stopped after three steps. His stomach still wanted grapes.

### Page 7

Finn looked back at the bird. "I wanted them," he said. "They were too high today."

### Page 8

The honest words felt better. Finn went to find food he could reach.

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Ari carried a little bell up the hill. Nell walked slowly beside him.

### Page 2

### Page 3

### Page 4

### Page 6

### Page 7

### Page 8

The bell stayed on the hill. It rang when the town needed help.

- Target vocabulary: crow, cup, stone, water, drop, plan
- High-frequency words: the, was, little, down, up, good
- Moral/theme: A careful plan can solve a hard problem.
- Source inspiration: Public-domain Aesop adaptation, rewritten by LiteracyPath

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Cora the crow was thirsty. She saw a blue cup by the garden wall.

### Page 2

The cup had water, but the water was far down. Cora could not reach it.

### Page 3

Cora looked at the path. Small stones sat in the dust.

### Page 4

She dropped one stone into the cup. The water moved up a little.

### Page 5

Cora dropped more stones. Plink, plink, plink went the cup.

### Page 6

The water came near the top. Cora took a cool drink.

### Page 7

A sparrow watched from the wall. "That was a good plan," he chirped.

### Page 8

Cora shook her wings. A small stone had helped in a big way.

## gs-b-05: The Small Boat

- Level: B
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Target literacy focus: CVC review, blends, long vowels, safety sequence
- Target vocabulary: boat, rope, dock, wave, safe, pull
- High-frequency words: the, my, said, come, down, here
- Moral/theme: Careful choices keep friends safe.
- Source inspiration: Original LiteracyPath story

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Tess saw a small blue boat by the dock. The boat bobbed in the water.

### Page 2

"Can I sit in it?" asked Tess. Uncle Jo held the rope.

### Page 3

"First, we check the vest," said Uncle Jo. Tess patted her yellow vest.

### Page 4

A little wave pushed the boat away. Tess reached out, but Uncle Jo shook his head.

### Page 5

"We pull the rope, not the boat," he said. Tess pulled slowly with both hands.

### Page 6

The boat came back to the dock. Uncle Jo tied the rope tight.

### Page 7

Now Tess could step in safely. Uncle Jo held her hand.

### Page 8

The small boat rocked softly. Tess smiled because safe steps made the ride fun.
