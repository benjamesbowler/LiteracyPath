## gs-c-01: The Lion and the Little Mouse

- Level: C
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Comprehension focus: Small actions can solve big problems.
- Phonics focus: short vowels, digraph review, simple dialogue
- Target vocabulary: lion, mouse, net, help, free, kind
- High-frequency words: the, said, little, went, help, was
- Moral/theme: Kindness matters, even when help seems small.
- Source inspiration: The Lion and the Mouse (Aesop's Fables)

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Luma the lion slept under a warm tree. Pip the mouse ran over his paw and stopped with a squeak.

### Page 2

Luma opened one eye and lifted his head. Pip said, “Please let me go, and I will help you one day.”

### Page 3

The lion almost laughed at the tiny mouse. Then he saw that Pip was brave, so he let him run home.

### Page 4

The next morning, hunters left a rope net in the tall grass. Luma stepped into it and could not get free.

### Page 5

Luma roared, but the net held tight. Birds flew up, and Pip heard the sound from his small home.

### Page 6

Pip ran to the net and saw the thick ropes. He did not run away, even though the ropes were big.

### Page 7

Pip bit one rope, then another rope. His small teeth worked slowly, but they worked well.

### Page 8

At last, the net opened wide. Luma shook his mane and stepped out carefully.

### Page 9

Luma bowed his great head to Pip. “You helped me,” said the lion, “just as you promised.”

### Page 10

Pip smiled and sat beside the lion’s paw. From that day on, Luma remembered that help can be small and strong.

## gs-c-02: The Crow and the Water Jar

- Level: C
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Comprehension focus: A character solves a problem by trying a plan.
- Phonics focus: short vowels, digraph review, simple dialogue
- Target vocabulary: crow, jar, stone, water, think, drink
- High-frequency words: the, said, little, went, help, was
- Moral/theme: Thinking carefully can help when strength is not enough.
- Source inspiration: The Crow and the Pitcher (Aesop's Fables)

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Cora the crow flew over a dry garden. Her throat felt scratchy, and she looked for water.

### Page 2

She found a tall clay jar beside a wall. Cora hopped close and saw water far down inside.

### Page 3

Cora pushed her beak into the jar. The water was too low, and she could not reach it.

### Page 4

She looked around the garden path. Small smooth stones lay near the wall.

### Page 5

Cora picked up one stone and dropped it into the jar. Plink! The water moved a little.

### Page 6

She dropped in another stone, then another. The water rose slowly, just like Cora hoped.

### Page 7

The sun was hot, but Cora did not quit. She carried stone after stone to the jar.

### Page 8

Soon the water was near the top. Cora leaned in and took a cool drink.

### Page 9

A little sparrow watched from the wall. “You made the water come up!” the sparrow chirped.

### Page 10

Cora shook her wings and smiled. “I did not make it come up,” she said. “I made a good plan.”

## gs-c-03: The Fox and the High Grapes

- Level: C
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Comprehension focus: Readers infer why the fox changes what he says.
- Phonics focus: short vowels, digraph review, simple dialogue
- Target vocabulary: fox, grapes, jump, high, try, honest
- High-frequency words: the, said, little, went, help, was
- Moral/theme: It is better to be honest about disappointment.
- Source inspiration: The Fox and the Grapes (Aesop's Fables)

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Felix the fox walked by a vine after lunch. Purple grapes hung high above his head.

### Page 2

The grapes looked round and sweet. Felix licked his lips and backed up for a jump.

### Page 3

He jumped once, but his paws touched only air. The grapes swung gently in the wind.

### Page 4

He jumped again and landed in a puff of dust. The grapes still waited high on the vine.

### Page 5

Felix looked left and right. No one had seen him miss, but his ears felt hot.

### Page 6

A bird called from the fence, “Those grapes look good.” Felix stood tall and flicked his tail.

### Page 7

“They are probably sour,” said Felix. “I did not want them anyway.”

### Page 8

Felix took three steps, then stopped. His stomach still wanted grapes, and his words felt wrong.

### Page 9

He looked back and said, “I wanted them, but they were too high for me today.” The honest words felt better than pretending.

### Page 10

The bird nodded from the fence. Felix felt lighter as he walked on to find lunch he could reach.

## gs-c-04: The Dog and the River Shadow

- Level: C
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Comprehension focus: Readers connect a choice to its consequence.
- Phonics focus: short vowels, digraph review, simple dialogue
- Target vocabulary: dog, river, shadow, bone, drop, want
- High-frequency words: the, said, little, went, help, was
- Moral/theme: Wanting more can make us lose what we have.
- Source inspiration: The Dog and His Shadow (Aesop's Fables)

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Rex the dog found a big treat near the bakery. He carried it proudly toward home.

### Page 2

To get home, Rex had to cross a small bridge. The river below was smooth and bright.

### Page 3

Rex looked down and saw another dog in the water. That dog had a treat too.

### Page 4

The dog in the water looked close. Rex thought, “If I take that treat, I will have two.”

### Page 5

Rex barked at the water dog. The treat fell from his mouth and splashed into the river.

### Page 6

The water dog vanished with the ripples. Rex stared at the empty river.

### Page 7

Rex sniffed the bridge boards. His treat was gone, and there was no other dog.

### Page 8

A baker waved from the path. “Rex, why do you look so sad?” she asked.

### Page 9

Rex wagged his tail slowly. He could not answer, but he knew he had made a poor choice.

### Page 10

From then on, Rex carried treats straight home. He did not bark at river shadows again.

## gs-c-06: The Bell in the Tree

- Level: C
- Type: fiction
- Status: draft_needs_assets
- Active: false
- Comprehension focus: Readers track clues that help a character solve a problem.
- Phonics focus: short vowels, digraph review, simple dialogue
- Target vocabulary: bell, tree, wind, listen, lost, home
- High-frequency words: the, said, little, went, help, was
- Moral/theme: Listening carefully can help us find the way.
- Source inspiration: Original LiteracyPath fable (LiteracyPath original guided stories)

### Full Page Text

### Page 1

Mina hung a little bell in the garden tree. Taro the dog watched the bell shine in the morning sun.

### Page 2

After lunch, Taro chased a leaf through the gate. The wind pushed the leaf down a quiet path.

### Page 3

Mina called for Taro, but the dog did not come. She stood still and listened to the windy garden.

### Page 4

Far away, she heard a tiny ring. The bell in the tree was moving in the wind.

### Page 5

Taro heard the bell too and lifted his ears. The sound told him which way led back.

### Page 6

Mina rang the bell again and again. Taro ran toward the sound with muddy paws.

### Page 7

Taro jumped into Mina’s arms. Mina laughed because the bell had become a helper.

### Page 8

The next day, Mina made a small rule for herself. She would close the gate before play.

### Page 9

Taro sat under the tree and listened. The bell was quiet now, but he knew its sound.

### Page 10

When the wind rang the bell, Mina smiled. It reminded her that careful listening can bring a friend home.
