# Fiction Continuity Checklist

Date: 2026-05-24

Use this checklist before any fiction Guided Story book can become active.

## Book-Level Checks

- The book has a complete character bible.
- The book has a complete environment bible.
- The book has continuity notes and a continuity status.
- The story has a coherent beginning, middle, and ending.
- Every page follows the previous page logically.
- No page feels like an unrelated image prompt or disconnected scene.

## Character Checks

- Main characters have the same appearance on every page.
- Hair, fur, feather, skin, or body colors remain identical.
- Eye color/style remains consistent.
- Clothing remains consistent unless the text clearly changes it.
- Accessories remain consistent and do not appear/disappear randomly.
- Species and visual age remain consistent.
- Height, body build, and proportions remain consistent.
- Character emotion follows the story sequence.
- Character speaking style matches the character bible.

## Setting Checks

- The primary setting remains stable unless the story explicitly moves.
- Indoor/outdoor location changes are explained by the text.
- Time of day is coherent.
- Weather is coherent.
- Lighting is coherent.
- Recurring props remain consistent.
- Background characters or objects do not change the story meaning.
- The visual style remains the same across all pages.

## Page-Level Checks

- The image matches the exact app text.
- The image shows the required action.
- The image shows only the required visible characters unless the prompt requests others.
- There is no embedded text, caption, watermark, label, or speech bubble.
- The page image looks like the next scene in the same book.
- The image does not redesign characters or settings for prettiness.

## Fiction Rejection Rules

Reject or regenerate a fiction page/book if:

- the main character changes appearance
- clothing randomly changes
- accessories randomly appear or disappear
- body proportions/species/age change
- the environment changes without story cause
- time of day/weather/lighting breaks continuity
- visual style changes heavily between pages
- story events do not flow sequentially
- the image is beautiful but contradicts the character bible or environment bible

## Nonfiction Distinction

Nonfiction pages may show different places, diagrams, examples, or factual scenes. Page-level relevance matters more than story continuity.

If a nonfiction book uses a recurring narrator, mascot, or child character, that recurring figure must still remain visually consistent.
